# PROTOTYPE_BRIEF_STRATEGY_STUDIO.md

## Direction
Strategy Studio

## Objective
Own the workflow from rough campaign ideas to structured annual plan.

## Design thesis
Marketing planning often starts messy. This direction should make the product strongest at shaping fuzzy ideas before dates are fully locked.

## Primary moment to optimize
A team is workshoping or pre-planning, turning tentative ideas into a coherent annual campaign map.

## Core emphasis
- strongest ideas/TBC experience
- more guided idea-to-schedule flow
- clearer distinction between unscheduled concepts and scheduled items
- expressive campaign shaping before timeline commitment

## Required prototype screens
1. project setup
2. ideas-heavy workspace state
3. idea-to-schedule conversion flow
4. default year view after scheduling
5. overlap stack state
6. export/review mode

## Interaction priorities
- ideas rail is first-class, not an afterthought
- converting ideas into scheduled items must feel frictionless
- campaign group creation should happen early and naturally
- timeline discipline must remain strong after scheduling

## Visual rules
- ideas should feel lightweight but persistent
- scheduling transition should be visually obvious
- campaign groups should bridge fuzzy thinking and time-based planning
- avoid becoming a brainstorm board with dates bolted on

## Success criteria
- users can capture and refine ideas without losing momentum
- the move from idea to structured plan feels better than in the other directions
- once scheduled, the plan still reads cleanly at annual scale

## Failure signs
- backlog becomes a dumping ground
- timeline feels secondary to ideation
- export/review mode loses clarity compared with Executive Canvas
